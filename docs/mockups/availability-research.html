The Headline: YouTube Does NOT Have Everything

Previous research assumed YouTube is universal. For standard album tracks, it is. But for bonus tracks, B-sides, remixes, demos, and deluxe-exclusive content — YouTube fails 81% of the time.

Revised Recommendation

YouTube is NOT universal for bonus content

The initial assumption that "YouTube has everything" is only true for standard album tracks. Deluxe bonus tracks, B-sides, demos, 5.1 mixes, and instrumentals are unavailable on YouTube 81% of the time.

What this means for the edition picker:

  • Standard editions → YT always works. Play immediately, upgrade to FLAC from torrent later.
  • Deluxe/expanded editions → Standard tracks play via YT. Bonus tracks will likely fail silently — no YT match, no torrent, nothing plays.
  • The user needs to know this BEFORE picking the deluxe edition — not after 10 tracks play fine and then 5 bonus tracks are silent.

Revised approach:

  • Approach 3 still valid — match existing torrent results to editions (free, no latency)
  • Add: distinguish "standard tracks" from "bonus tracks" in the edition view. Standard tracks = always playable. Bonus tracks = may not be available from any source.
  • Don't hide editions — but visually separate the bonus content from the core album
  • Graceful failure — when a bonus track can't be found on YT, show "Not available" instead of silently skipping

Data source

50 albums selected from Nathan's Spotify listening history (86K plays, 17K unique albums). Each album queried against MusicBrainz for editions, then bonus tracks tested against the live staging YouTube search API. All data collected 2026-03-28.
